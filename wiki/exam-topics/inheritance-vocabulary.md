# Inheritance vocabulary

## Answer
- **Inheritance** — the passing of genetic traits from parents to offspring.
- **Gene** — a sequence of DNA that codes for a specific protein (or RNA product); the basic unit of heredity.
- **Allele** — an alternative version of a gene. Diploid cells carry two alleles per gene (one on each homologous chromosome).
- **Homozygous** — both alleles for a gene are the same (e.g. YY or yy). _Homozygous dominant_ = two dominant alleles; _homozygous recessive_ = two recessive alleles.
- **Heterozygous** — one dominant and one recessive allele (e.g. Yy). Heterozygotes can be **carriers** of recessive disease alleles without showing the trait.
- **Dominant** — an allele whose effect appears whenever it is present (one copy is enough).
- **Recessive** — an allele whose effect is masked when a dominant allele is present; usually encodes a non-functional protein.
- **Polygenic** — a trait controlled by multiple genes (e.g. skin color, height). Produces continuous variation.
- **Pleiotropic** — a single gene that has **multiple, seemingly unrelated effects** on the phenotype (e.g. Marfan syndrome).
