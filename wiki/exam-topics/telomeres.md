# Telomeres and the replication problem

## Answer
A **telomere** is a region of repetitive DNA sequence (in humans: TTAGGG repeats) at the **ends of linear chromosomes**. Telomeres protect the chromosome from being read as broken DNA and from being degraded.

**Why replicating the end is difficult**: the **lagging strand** needs an RNA primer to start each Okazaki fragment. At the very end of the chromosome, after the last RNA primer is removed, there's no upstream 3'-OH for DNA polymerase to extend onto, so the gap can't be filled. **The chromosome shortens by a small amount with each round of replication.**

This problem only affects the lagging strand of linear chromosomes. Prokaryotes don't have it (their DNA is circular).
