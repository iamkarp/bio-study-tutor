# Transcription termination

## Answer
At the **terminator** (a specific DNA sequence at the end of a gene), RNA polymerase, the new RNA strand, and the DNA template all **separate**.
- The **DNA** re-forms a continuous double helix.
- The **RNA** is processed in the nucleus (5' cap added, poly-A tail added, introns spliced out → mature mRNA), then leaves the nucleus through nuclear pores.
- The mature mRNA travels to the **cytoplasm** where it can be translated by a ribosome.
