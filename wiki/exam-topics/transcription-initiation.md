# Transcription initiation

## Answer
**Initiation** is the first step of transcription. The enzyme **[[enzymes/rna-polymerase|RNA polymerase]]** binds to the **[[structures/promoter|promoter]]** — the DNA region at the **start of a gene** that signals "transcription begins here". In eukaryotes, RNA polymerase needs **transcription factors** to bind the promoter first; the transcription factors then recruit RNA polymerase. Once bound, RNA polymerase **unwinds the DNA double helix** locally to expose the template strand, ready for elongation.
