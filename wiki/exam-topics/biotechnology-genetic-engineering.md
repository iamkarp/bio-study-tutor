# Biotechnology and genetic engineering

## Answer
- **Biotechnology** — the use of living organisms or their molecular components (DNA, RNA, proteins) to make products or carry out processes useful to humans. Includes everything from PCR and DNA fingerprinting to GMO crops and gene therapy.
- **Genetic engineering** — a subset of biotechnology that involves the **deliberate alteration of an organism's genome** (inserting, deleting, or modifying DNA sequences). Includes recombinant DNA, CRISPR gene editing, and the production of genetically modified organisms.

In short: all genetic engineering is biotechnology, but not all biotechnology is genetic engineering.
