# What is gene linkage?

## Answer
**Gene linkage**: genes that sit **close together on the same chromosome** tend to be inherited as a unit — they don't sort independently. The closer they are, the more likely they are inherited together.

This is an **exception to** [[principles/law-of-independent-assortment]], which only applies to genes on different chromosomes (or genes very far apart on the same chromosome). Linked genes give offspring ratios that deviate from the expected 9:3:3:1 dihybrid pattern.
