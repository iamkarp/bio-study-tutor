# Introns and exons

## Answer
- **Exons** = "**ex**pressed" segments — they **stay in the mature mRNA** and code for the protein.
- **Introns** = "**in**tervening" segments — they're transcribed into pre-mRNA but **spliced out** in the nucleus and **never leave** as part of the message.

Pre-mRNA contains both, alternating like beads on a string. **Splicing** by the spliceosome removes introns and joins the remaining exons into mature mRNA, which then exits the nucleus and is translated.

**Functional value of intron–exon structure**: the same gene can be spliced different ways, joining different combinations of exons (**alternative splicing**) to make multiple proteins from one gene.
