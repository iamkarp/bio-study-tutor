# DNA replication: mitosis vs. meiosis

## Answer
**No — the replication mechanism is the same**. In both cases, DNA is replicated **once during S phase** of interphase, using the same enzymes (helicase, primase, DNA polymerase III, etc.) and producing two identical sister chromatids per chromosome.

The difference is in what happens **after**:
- **Mitosis** — one round of division separates sister chromatids → 2 diploid cells.
- **Meiosis** — two rounds of division (no replication between them); meiosis I separates homologues, meiosis II separates sister chromatids → 4 haploid cells.
