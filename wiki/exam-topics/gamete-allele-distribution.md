# How are alleles distributed into gametes?

## Answer
During **meiosis I**, the two members of a homologous pair separate, so each gamete receives **only one** of the two alleles for any given gene.
- A **homozygous parent (PP or pp)** can only make one kind of gamete: P or p, respectively.
- A **heterozygous parent (Pp)** makes **two kinds** of gametes in **equal proportions** — half carry P, half carry p.

This is the physical basis of [[principles/law-of-segregation]]: the two alleles for one gene end up in separate gametes.
