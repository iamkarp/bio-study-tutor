# Sex-linked Punnett squares (diseased female × normal male)

## Answer
For an **X-linked recessive** disease, let X^h = recessive disease allele, X^H = normal dominant allele, Y = Y chromosome (carries no copy).

**Cross**: diseased female (X^h X^h) × normal male (X^H Y).

|  | X^h | X^h |
|---|---|---|
| **X^H** | X^H X^h | X^H X^h |
| **Y** | X^h Y | X^h Y |

**Offspring**:
- **All daughters (X^H X^h)** — heterozygous **carriers**, phenotypically normal but able to pass the disease allele to sons.
- **All sons (X^h Y)** — **diseased** (males have only one X, so any recessive X-linked allele is expressed).

**Take-home**: males can never be carriers for X-linked recessive disease — they're either affected or not.
