# Given a DNA sequence, write the complementary strand

## Answer
**Rules**:
- A pairs with T, G pairs with C (DNA-DNA pairing).
- Strands are **antiparallel**: write the complement in the **opposite 5'/3' direction**.

**Worked example**: original strand 5'-ATGCCATGC-3'

| Original (5' → 3') | A | T | G | C | C | A | T | G | C |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Complement (3' → 5') | T | A | C | G | G | T | A | C | G |

So the complementary strand is **3'-TACGGTACG-5'** (or 5'-GCATGGCAT-3' if rewritten in conventional 5'→3' direction).

For DNA → RNA pairing (transcription), substitute **U for T** in the new RNA strand.
