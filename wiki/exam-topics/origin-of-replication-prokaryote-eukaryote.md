# Origin of replication — prokaryotes vs. eukaryotes

## Answer
An **origin of replication (ORI)** is a specific DNA sequence where replication begins. Helicase opens the helix here, forming a **replication bubble** with two replication forks moving in opposite directions.

| | Prokaryotes (e.g. bacteria) | Eukaryotes (e.g. humans) |
|---|---|---|
| DNA shape | One large **circular** chromosome (+ smaller plasmids) | Multiple **linear** chromosomes |
| ORIs per chromosome | **One** | **Many** |
| Replication bubbles | One | Many — they expand and merge |

Why eukaryotes need multiple ORIs: their chromosomes are far longer, and a single origin would take too long to copy.
