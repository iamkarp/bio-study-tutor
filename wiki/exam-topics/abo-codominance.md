# ABO blood typing and codominance

## Answer
The **I gene** has three alleles:
- **I^A** — encodes the A antigen on red blood cells (functional protein, dominant)
- **I^B** — encodes the B antigen (functional protein, dominant)
- **i** — recessive, encodes a non-functional version (no antigen)

I^A and I^B are **codominant**: when both are present, **both proteins are produced** and **both antigens appear** on the cell surface — neither masks the other.

| Genotype | Phenotype (blood type) |
|---|---|
| I^A I^A or I^A i | A |
| I^B I^B or I^B i | B |
| **I^A I^B** | **AB** (codominance) |
| ii | O |

**Key distinction**: in [[inheritance/incomplete-dominance|incomplete dominance]] you get a **blended** phenotype (pink); in **codominance** you get **both phenotypes simultaneously** (both A and B antigens, not a blend).
