# Where are Okazaki fragments found?

## Answer
Okazaki fragments are found **only on the [[structures/lagging-strand|lagging strand]]** during DNA replication. They're the short DNA pieces (each ~100–200 nucleotides in eukaryotes; ~1000–2000 in prokaryotes) created because the lagging strand has to be synthesized **away from** the replication fork in segments. After DNA polymerase I replaces their RNA primers and DNA ligase seals the gaps, the fragments become a continuous strand.
