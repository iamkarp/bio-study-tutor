# What is crossing over and when does it occur?

## Answer
**Crossing over** is the exchange of DNA segments between two **homologous chromosomes** that occurs during **prophase I of meiosis**. The exchange happens at sites called **chiasmata** (typically 2–3 per pair in human cells).

**The point**: crossing over generates **genetic recombination** — new allele combinations that did not exist on either parental chromosome. Combined with [[principles/law-of-independent-assortment]], it is the major source of genetic variation among gametes (and therefore among offspring).
