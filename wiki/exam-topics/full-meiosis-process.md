# Explain the entire process of meiosis

## Answer
**Starting cell**: 1 diploid cell with 2n chromosomes (e.g. 46 in humans). DNA has been **replicated once during S phase** of interphase, so each chromosome consists of two sister chromatids.

**Meiosis I — reductive division** (separates homologous pairs):
1. **Prophase I** — Homologues pair up; **crossing over** at chiasmata creates recombinant chromatids. Nuclear envelope breaks down; spindle forms.
2. **Metaphase I** — Homologous pairs line up at the equator; orientation of each pair is **independent** (independent assortment).
3. **Anaphase I** — Homologues separate; sister chromatids stay together.
4. **Telophase I** — Two haploid cells form, each with n duplicated chromosomes.

**Meiosis II — equational division** (separates sister chromatids; no DNA replication between):
5. **Prophase II** — Spindle re-forms.
6. **Metaphase II** — Duplicated chromosomes line up singly.
7. **Anaphase II** — Sister chromatids separate.
8. **Telophase II** — Four haploid cells form, each with n single chromosomes.

**End result**: 1 diploid cell → **4 genetically distinct haploid gametes**, each carrying a unique mixture of maternal and paternal alleles thanks to crossing over and independent assortment.
