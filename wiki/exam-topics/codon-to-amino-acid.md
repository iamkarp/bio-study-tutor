# Reading the codon table

## Answer
A **codon** is a 3-nucleotide sequence on mRNA that specifies one amino acid. To go from RNA → protein, read the mRNA in **groups of three** starting from the **start codon (AUG)** and use the **codon chart** to look up each codon's amino acid until you reach a **stop codon (UAA, UAG, UGA)**.

The exam will provide a codon chart. Reminders:
- Read **codons on mRNA**, not on DNA. If given a DNA template, transcribe to RNA first (substitute U for T, and complement the template).
- Read **5' → 3'** on the mRNA.
- The chart usually orders codons by 1st base (rows), 2nd base (columns), 3rd base (sub-rows).

**Codon redundancy**: most amino acids are encoded by more than one codon (the "third position wobble"). This is why some point mutations are silent.
