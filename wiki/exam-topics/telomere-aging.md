# Telomere length and aging

## Answer
**Telomeres shorten with age.** Most somatic (body) cells lack [[enzymes/telomerase|telomerase]], so each round of cell division clips a bit off the end of each chromosome. Eventually telomeres reach a critical minimum length and the cell stops dividing (cellular senescence) — Greider proposed that the **maximum number of cell divisions is set by the initial telomere length**. In cell culture, telomere length and division count correlate.

This is one molecular mechanism that contributes to aging at the cellular level.
