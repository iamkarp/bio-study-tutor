# Bio 1320 Exam 3 — High-Level Map

The exam covers chapters 9–13. Five themes anchor the material:

## 1. Meiosis (Ch 9) — How Cells Make Gametes
[[chapter:09]]

A diploid cell goes through **one round of DNA replication** then **two rounds of division** — [[processes/meiosis-i]] separates homologous pairs, [[processes/meiosis-ii]] separates sister chromatids — yielding **four haploid gametes**. Variability comes from [[processes/crossing-over]] (prophase I) and [[principles/law-of-independent-assortment]] (metaphase I). Errors of separation are called [[processes/nondisjunction]] and produce disorders like [[diseases/down-syndrome]].

**Key comparison**: [[comparisons/mitosis-vs-meiosis]].

## 2. Inheritance (Ch 10) — How Traits Are Passed
[[chapter:10]]

[[people/gregor-mendel|Mendel]]'s pea experiments revealed that traits come in **discrete units (alleles)** and follow two laws: [[principles/law-of-segregation]] and [[principles/law-of-independent-assortment]]. Use [[techniques/punnett-square|Punnett squares]] to predict offspring genotypes/phenotypes. Beyond simple Mendelian patterns: [[inheritance/incomplete-dominance]] (blended), [[inheritance/codominance]] (both expressed — [[concepts/abo-blood-typing]]), [[inheritance/sex-linked]] (more affected males — [[concepts/x-inactivation]] complicates things in females), [[concepts/gene-linkage]] (closely linked genes don't sort independently), [[concepts/environment-and-phenotype]] (Siamese cat fur).

## 3. DNA Structure & Replication (Ch 11) — The Molecule of Heredity
[[chapter:11]]

[[experiments/griffith-experiment|Griffith]] showed something inheritable could transform bacteria; that something turned out to be DNA. DNA is a **[[structures/double-helix]]** of [[molecules/dna|deoxyribonucleotides]] — a [[structures/sugar-phosphate-backbone]] (exterior) with paired [[structures/nitrogenous-base|bases]] (interior, A-T and G-C, [[principles/complementary-base-pairing]]). Replication is **[[principles/semiconservative-replication|semiconservative]]**, runs 5'→3' on the [[structures/leading-strand]] and in [[structures/okazaki-fragment|fragments]] on the [[structures/lagging-strand]], and uses many enzymes ([[enzymes/dna-helicase|helicase]], [[enzymes/topoisomerase|topoisomerase]], [[enzymes/rna-primase|primase]], [[enzymes/dna-polymerase-iii|DNA pol III]], [[enzymes/dna-polymerase-i|DNA pol I]], [[enzymes/dna-ligase|ligase]]). Linear chromosomes shorten at each division — [[structures/telomere|telomeres]] and [[enzymes/telomerase]] manage this.

## 4. How Genes Work (Ch 12) — DNA → RNA → Protein
[[chapter:12]]

The [[principles/central-dogma]] in two steps:
- **[[processes/transcription]]** — [[enzymes/rna-polymerase]] copies a gene into [[molecules/mrna]] (in the nucleus). Eukaryotic transcripts are processed: 5' cap, poly-A tail, and [[processes/splicing|splicing]] of [[structures/intron|introns]] out of [[structures/exon|exons]].
- **[[processes/translation]]** — at a [[structures/ribosome]], [[molecules/trna|tRNAs]] match anticodons to mRNA [[molecules/codon|codons]] (3 nucleotides → 1 amino acid). Starts at AUG ([[molecules/methionine]]); ends at a [[principles/stop-codon|stop codon]].

[[concepts/gene-regulation]] in eukaryotes operates at chromatin, transcription, splicing, mRNA stability, and post-translational levels. [[concepts/mutation|Mutations]] (substitution, insertion, deletion, [[concepts/frameshift-mutation|frameshift]]) can be silent or catastrophic — [[diseases/sickle-cell-anemia]] is a single-base missense.

## 5. New Biology (Ch 13) — Manipulating DNA
[[chapter:13]]

[[concepts/biotechnology]] toolkit:
- **[[techniques/pcr]]** — exponentially amplifies a target sequence. Components: target DNA, [[enzymes/dna-polymerase|heat-stable polymerase]], [[molecules/dntp|dNTPs]], [[molecules/primer|primers]]. Cycles: [[processes/pcr-denaturation|denature]] (95 °C) → [[processes/pcr-annealing|anneal]] (55 °C) → [[processes/pcr-extension|extend]] (72 °C). Each cycle doubles.
- **[[techniques/gel-electrophoresis]]** — separates DNA fragments by size.
- **[[techniques/dna-sequencing]]** — reads the order of bases.
- **[[techniques/str-analysis]]** — 13 [[concepts/short-tandem-repeat|STR]] loci → DNA profile (1-in-a-trillion uniqueness). Used to identify suspects, paternity, and remains.
- **[[concepts/human-genome-project]]** — sequenced all 3 billion human bases by 2003.

[[people/kary-mullis|Kary Mullis]] invented PCR; Nobel 1993.
