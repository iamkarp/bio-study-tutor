# Sex-Linked Inheritance

## Summary
Inheritance of genes located on the **sex chromosomes** (usually the X — the much larger one). Most "sex-linked" disorders are **X-linked recessive** (e.g. color blindness, hemophilia, Duchenne muscular dystrophy).

Key consequence:
- **Males (XY)** have only one X — they can't be carriers; any X-linked recessive allele is expressed.
- **Females (XX)** can be **carriers** (heterozygous) — phenotypically normal but able to pass the disease to sons.

This makes X-linked recessive disorders **far more common in males**.
