# Codominance

## Summary
A non-Mendelian inheritance pattern in which **both alleles are fully expressed** in the heterozygote — neither masks the other. Both proteins are produced and both phenotypes are visible **simultaneously** (not blended).

Classic example: **ABO blood typing**. The I^A and I^B alleles are codominant — heterozygotes (I^A I^B) have **both** A and B antigens on their red blood cells, giving blood type AB. See [[concepts/abo-blood-typing]].
