# Primer

## Summary
A short single-stranded nucleic acid that provides a **free 3'-OH** for [[enzymes/dna-polymerase-iii|DNA polymerase]] to extend.

- **In DNA replication** — RNA primers (~5–10 nt) laid down by [[enzymes/rna-primase]]. Required because DNA polymerase cannot start a new chain from scratch. Later replaced with DNA by [[enzymes/dna-polymerase-i]].
- **In PCR** — DNA primers (~20 nt) designed to flank the target sequence. They define **what gets amplified** and provide a starting point for the heat-stable polymerase.
