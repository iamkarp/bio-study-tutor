# Chapter 12 — How Genes Work

## Summary
Chapter 12 covers the central dogma: DNA is transcribed into mRNA, which is translated into protein at ribosomes. RNA differs from DNA (single-stranded, ribose, uracil instead of thymine); three RNA types (mRNA, rRNA, tRNA) cooperate. Pre-mRNA is processed (5' cap, poly-A tail, splicing of introns/exons) before leaving the nucleus. Translation reads codons three nucleotides at a time, starts with methionine, and ends at a stop codon. Eukaryotes regulate gene expression at multiple levels (chromatin, transcription factors, splicing, mRNA stability, microRNA, post-translational). Mutations include substitution, insertion, deletion, and frameshift.
