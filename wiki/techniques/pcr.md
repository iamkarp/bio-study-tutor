# Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)

## Summary
A laboratory technique for **rapidly amplifying** a specific DNA sequence — taking a few molecules and producing **billions of copies** in a few hours. Invented by **[[people/kary-mullis|Kary Mullis]]** (1983, Nobel Prize 1993).

## Components
- **Target DNA** (the template)
- **Heat-stable [[enzymes/dna-polymerase]]** (e.g. Taq from *Thermus aquaticus*)
- **dNTPs** (free nucleotides as building blocks)
- **Primers** — two short DNA sequences that flank the target region

## Steps (per cycle)
1. **[[processes/pcr-denaturation|Denaturation]]** at ~95 °C — strands separate (H-bonds break)
2. **[[processes/pcr-annealing|Annealing]]** at ~55 °C — primers bind to single-stranded templates
3. **[[processes/pcr-extension|Extension]]** at ~72 °C — polymerase synthesizes new DNA from primers

Each cycle **doubles** the target copies. After n cycles: 2ⁿ copies. 30 cycles → ~10⁹ copies — visualizable on a gel.

## Applications
Forensics ([[techniques/str-analysis|STR profiling]]), paternity testing, infectious disease diagnosis, ancient DNA, gene cloning, COVID testing.
