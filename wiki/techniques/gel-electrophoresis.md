# Gel Electrophoresis

## Summary
A laboratory technique for **separating DNA fragments by size**.

## How It Works
1. DNA samples are loaded into wells in an **agarose gel** (porous polymer matrix).
2. An **electric current** is applied across the gel. DNA's [[structures/phosphate-group|phosphate backbone]] is **negatively charged**, so DNA migrates toward the **positive electrode**.
3. **Smaller fragments move faster** through the porous gel; larger fragments lag.
4. Over time the fragments separate into bands at different distances from the wells.
5. Stain (ethidium bromide, SYBR Safe) and image under UV.

In **[[techniques/str-analysis|STR profiling]]**, the band pattern is the DNA profile — comparing crime-scene to suspect samples either matches or excludes.
