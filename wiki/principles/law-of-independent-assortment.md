# Law of Independent Assortment (Mendel's Second Law)

## Summary
**The alleles for one gene segregate into gametes independently of the alleles for another gene.**

Cellular mechanism: at [[processes/metaphase-i]], each homologous pair orients **randomly** on the spindle relative to every other pair. With 23 pairs of chromosomes, this gives 2²³ ≈ 8.4 million unique chromosome combinations per gamete (before considering crossing over).

**Caveat**: only true for genes on **different chromosomes** (or genes far apart on the same chromosome). Genes that sit close together on the same chromosome are **[[concepts/gene-linkage|linked]]** and tend to be inherited together — though [[processes/crossing-over]] can break that linkage.
