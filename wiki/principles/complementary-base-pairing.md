# Complementary Base Pairing

## Summary
The principle that DNA bases pair **specifically and exclusively**:
- **A pairs with T** (2 hydrogen bonds) in DNA; A pairs with U in RNA
- **G pairs with C** (3 hydrogen bonds)

Each strand of DNA therefore **dictates** the sequence of its partner — a sequence on one strand uniquely defines the complement.

This principle is the basis of:
- [[processes/dna-replication]] — new strand is complementary to template
- [[processes/transcription]] — RNA is complementary to template DNA
- [[processes/translation]] — anticodon is complementary to codon
- [[techniques/pcr]] — primers anneal to complementary regions
