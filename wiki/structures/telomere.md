# Telomere

## Summary
A region of **repetitive DNA at the end of a linear chromosome** (in humans, the sequence is **TTAGGG** repeated thousands of times). Telomeres protect chromosome ends from being recognized as DNA breaks and from degradation.

Because of the [[structures/lagging-strand]] problem, **a small portion of telomere DNA is lost with every cell division** in cells that lack [[enzymes/telomerase|telomerase]]. Telomere shortening contributes to cellular aging and limits the number of times a somatic cell can divide.

## Related
- [[enzymes/telomerase]] — extends telomeres in germ cells, stem cells, and cancer cells.
- Prokaryotes don't have this problem because their DNA is circular.
