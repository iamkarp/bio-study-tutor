# Antiparallel Strands

## Summary
The two DNA strands run in **opposite 5' → 3' directions**. One strand goes 5' → 3' "up" while the other goes 5' → 3' "down" relative to it.

This matters because [[enzymes/dna-polymerase-iii|DNA polymerase]] can only build new strands in the 5' → 3' direction. With antiparallel templates, only one of the two strands can be synthesized continuously toward a replication fork ([[structures/leading-strand]]); the other ([[structures/lagging-strand]]) is built in fragments.

- **5' end** — has a free phosphate group on the 5' carbon of the sugar.
- **3' end** — has a free –OH on the 3' carbon of the sugar.
