# Okazaki Fragment

## Summary
A short stretch of newly synthesized DNA on the [[structures/lagging-strand]]. Each fragment is started by an RNA primer laid down by [[enzymes/rna-primase]] and extended 5' → 3' by [[enzymes/dna-polymerase-iii]] until it reaches the previous fragment's primer. Then [[enzymes/dna-polymerase-i]] replaces the RNA primer with DNA, and [[enzymes/dna-ligase]] seals the gap. Eukaryotic Okazaki fragments are ~100–200 nt; prokaryotic are ~1000–2000 nt.
