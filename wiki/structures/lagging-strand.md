# Lagging Strand

## Summary
The strand that must be synthesized **away from the [[structures/replication-fork|replication fork]]** because of the 5' → 3' rule. To accomplish this, the lagging strand is built in **short pieces** called [[structures/okazaki-fragment|Okazaki fragments]]. Each fragment requires its own RNA primer (laid down by [[enzymes/rna-primase]]); [[enzymes/dna-polymerase-i]] later replaces those primers with DNA, and [[enzymes/dna-ligase]] seals the gaps.

The lagging strand is also why linear chromosomes shorten with each replication ([[structures/telomere]] problem).
