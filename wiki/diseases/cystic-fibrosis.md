# Cystic Fibrosis

## Summary
An **autosomal-recessive** genetic disorder caused by mutations in the **CFTR gene** on chromosome 7. CFTR encodes a chloride channel; the mutated version produces thick mucus that clogs the lungs and pancreas. Carriers (heterozygotes) are unaffected; only homozygous-recessive individuals show the disease.

Used in the chapter as an example of using **Punnett squares** to track genetic disorder inheritance.
