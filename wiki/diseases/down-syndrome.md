# Down Syndrome

## Summary
A chromosomal disorder caused by **three copies of chromosome 21 (trisomy 21)** instead of the usual two. The extra chromosome arises from **[[processes/nondisjunction]]** during meiosis (most often in the egg). Affects ~1 in 700 births.

Phenotypic features include intellectual disability, characteristic facial features, increased risk of heart defects and early-onset Alzheimer's. Risk increases sharply with maternal age.
