# Anaphase I

## Summary
**Homologous chromosomes separate** and move to opposite poles. Sister chromatids **remain attached** at the centromere — they don't separate until anaphase II. This is the key mechanical difference from mitotic anaphase, where sister chromatids separate immediately.

If [[processes/nondisjunction]] occurs here, all four eventual gametes are abnormal (2 with extra, 2 missing chromosomes).
