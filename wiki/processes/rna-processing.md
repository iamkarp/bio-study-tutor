# RNA Processing

## Summary
In eukaryotes, the initial transcript ([[concepts/pre-mrna|pre-mRNA]]) is processed in the nucleus before becoming mature mRNA. Three modifications:

1. **5' cap** ([[structures/mrna-cap]]) — modified guanine added to 5' end; helps ribosome recognition and protects from degradation.
2. **3' poly-A tail** ([[structures/poly-a-tail]]) — long string of adenines added; stabilizes mRNA and aids nuclear export.
3. **Splicing** ([[processes/splicing]]) — [[structures/intron|introns]] are excised; [[structures/exon|exons]] are joined together.

The mature mRNA then exits the nucleus through nuclear pores into the cytoplasm.
