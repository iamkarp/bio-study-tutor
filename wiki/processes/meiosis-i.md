# Meiosis I

## Summary
The first of meiosis's two divisions. **Meiosis I separates homologous chromosomes** (one of each pair to each daughter cell), reducing chromosome number from diploid to haploid. Sister chromatids stay attached.

## Steps
1. [[processes/prophase-i]] — homologues pair, [[processes/crossing-over]] occurs at chiasmata
2. [[processes/metaphase-i]] — pairs line up at the equator with random orientation ([[principles/law-of-independent-assortment]])
3. [[processes/anaphase-i]] — homologues separate (sister chromatids stay together)
4. [[processes/telophase-i]] — two haploid cells form, each with duplicated chromosomes

## Outcome
2 haploid daughter cells, each with **n duplicated chromosomes**. (Starting from 2n=46 in humans, each daughter has 23 chromosomes still composed of two sister chromatids.)
