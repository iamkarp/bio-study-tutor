# Metaphase I

## Summary
**Homologous chromosome pairs** line up at the cell equator. Each homologue is attached by spindle microtubules to the **opposite pole** from its partner. Maternal/paternal orientation is **random** across pairs — this is the cellular basis of [[principles/law-of-independent-assortment]].

## Compared To
- [[processes/metaphase-ii]] — duplicated chromosomes line up singly
- Mitotic metaphase — also singles, but in diploid cells

See [[comparisons/mitosis-vs-meiosis]] and [[exam-topics/metaphase-comparison]].
