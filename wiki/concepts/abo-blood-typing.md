# ABO Blood Typing

## Summary
Human blood types are determined by the **I gene**, which has **three alleles**:
- **I^A** — dominant, encodes A antigen
- **I^B** — dominant, encodes B antigen
- **i** — recessive, encodes nothing functional

I^A and I^B are **codominant** to each other; both are dominant over i.

| Genotype | Phenotype |
|---|---|
| I^A I^A or I^A i | Type A |
| I^B I^B or I^B i | Type B |
| **I^A I^B** | **Type AB** (codominance) |
| ii | Type O |

A useful counter-example to incomplete dominance: in AB blood, both A and B antigens are present **simultaneously** — not blended.
