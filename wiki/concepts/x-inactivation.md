# X-Inactivation

## Summary
A mechanism for **dosage compensation**: in each cell of an XX individual, **one of the two X chromosomes is randomly silenced** ("inactivated") early in development and condensed into a Barr body. This prevents double-dosing of X-linked gene products.

**Random inactivation** means each cell expresses different alleles. In **calico cats**, this produces patches of orange and black fur where each patch descends from a cell that inactivated one or the other X. In humans, X-inactivation explains the variable expression of [[diseases/rett-syndrome|Rett syndrome]] in girls — the more cells that silence the Rett-carrying X, the milder the disease.
