# X-Linked Recessive

## Summary
A specific [[inheritance/sex-linked]] pattern in which the recessive allele is on the X chromosome. **Affects more males than females**: females need to be homozygous (X^h X^h) to be affected, but males express any X-linked recessive allele they have (X^h Y) because their Y carries no copy of the gene.

Standard pattern in pedigrees: trait skips generations through carrier females; affected males have unaffected (carrier) mothers; affected fathers cannot pass the trait to sons (sons get the father's Y, not X).
