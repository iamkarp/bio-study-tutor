# Human Genome Project (1990–2003)

## Summary
An international research effort to **determine the complete nucleotide sequence of all human DNA** (~3 billion bases). Launched 1990, finished 2003 with **99.99% accuracy**. Findings:

- ~**19,949 human genes** (far fewer than the 100,000+ predicted before)
- **Genes make up only ~2% of the genome** — the rest is non-coding
- Many new disease-associated genes were discovered; the function of most non-coding DNA is still uncertain

**Why it mattered**: knowing a gene's nucleotide sequence allows prediction of its protein's amino acid sequence, which can then be compared with known proteins to infer function. Provides the foundation for personalized medicine, [[concepts/gene-therapy]], and modern genomic biology.
