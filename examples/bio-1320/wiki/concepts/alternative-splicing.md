# Alternative Splicing

## Summary
The process by which **a single gene can produce multiple protein isoforms** by joining different combinations of [[structures/exon|exons]] during [[processes/splicing]]. By skipping or including different exons, a cell can make several related-but-distinct proteins from one pre-mRNA. About 95% of human multi-exon genes undergo alternative splicing — it's a major mechanism of [[concepts/gene-regulation]].
