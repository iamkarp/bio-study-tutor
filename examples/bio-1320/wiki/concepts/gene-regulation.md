# Gene Regulation

## Summary
The control of when, where, and how much a gene is expressed. Eukaryotes regulate at multiple levels:
1. **Chromatin** — DNA wound on histones; tightly wound = silent.
2. **Transcription factors** — required for RNA polymerase to bind promoter.
3. **Alternative splicing** — different exon combinations → different proteins.
4. **mRNA export** — proteins can hold mRNA in the nucleus.
5. **mRNA stability** — quick degradation; **microRNA** silencing.
6. **Post-translational** — folding, modifications, targeted degradation.

Regulation is essential because protein synthesis is energetically expensive and because different cell types must express different gene sets despite sharing the same genome.
