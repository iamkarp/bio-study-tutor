# Gene Therapy

## Summary
A medical approach that treats genetic disease by **introducing, modifying, or replacing genes** in a patient's cells. Approaches include adding a working copy of a defective gene, silencing a harmful gene, or directly editing the genome (CRISPR). Used (in trials and approved therapies) for some inherited blindness, sickle cell disease, spinal muscular atrophy, and certain cancers.
