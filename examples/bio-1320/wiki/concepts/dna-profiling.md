# DNA Profiling

## Summary
A forensic technique that **identifies individuals by their unique DNA pattern**, typically using [[techniques/str-analysis|STR analysis]]. The standard approach amplifies 13 STR loci with [[techniques/pcr]] and visualizes the results on [[techniques/gel-electrophoresis]]. The probability of two unrelated people having identical profiles across all 13 STRs is about **1 in a trillion**.

Used to identify suspects, exonerate the wrongly convicted, establish paternity, and identify human remains. Famous case: the Golden State Killer (Joseph James DeAngelo).
