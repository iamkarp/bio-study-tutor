# Griffith's Experiment (1928)

## Summary
**Frederick Griffith** worked with two strains of *Streptococcus pneumoniae*:
- **Smooth (S) strain** — virulent (kills mice)
- **Rough (R) strain** — harmless

He observed:
1. Live S → mouse dies
2. Live R → mouse lives
3. Heat-killed S → mouse lives (confirms S is dead)
4. **Heat-killed S + live R → mouse dies**, and live S bacteria are recovered from the dead mouse

**Conclusion**: something from the dead S bacteria **transformed** the harmless R bacteria into the virulent type. That "something" — later identified as **DNA** by Avery, MacLeod, and McCarty in 1944 — is the **molecule of heredity**.
