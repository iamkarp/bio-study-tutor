# Replication Fork

## Summary
The **Y-shaped structure** where the DNA double helix is being unwound during replication — one side is the still-paired parental duplex, the other is the two separated strands being copied. **[[enzymes/dna-helicase|Helicase]]** does the unwinding at the fork; replication proceeds outward from the [[structures/origin-of-replication]].

The two strands at the fork have to be replicated differently because of their antiparallel orientation: one becomes the [[structures/leading-strand]] (continuous, toward the fork) and the other becomes the [[structures/lagging-strand]] (in [[structures/okazaki-fragment|Okazaki fragments]], away from the fork).
