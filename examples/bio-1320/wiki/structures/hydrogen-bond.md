# Hydrogen Bond

## Summary
The weak non-covalent attraction holding **complementary base pairs** together inside the [[structures/double-helix]]. **A–T pairs share 2 H-bonds; G–C pairs share 3 H-bonds**, so G–C-rich regions are slightly harder to separate. Hydrogen bonds are weak enough to be broken thermally — that's why heat (e.g. 95 °C in [[techniques/pcr|PCR]] denaturation) separates DNA strands without breaking the covalent backbone.
