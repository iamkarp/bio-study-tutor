# Nitrogenous Base

## Summary
The information-carrying part of a [[molecules/dna|DNA]] nucleotide. There are four in DNA: **[[molecules/adenine|adenine (A)]], [[molecules/thymine|thymine (T)]], [[molecules/guanine|guanine (G)]], [[molecules/cytosine|cytosine (C)]]**. In RNA, **uracil (U)** replaces thymine.

- **Purines** (double ring): A, G
- **Pyrimidines** (single ring): C, T (and U)

A purine always pairs with a pyrimidine: **A–T (2 H-bonds)**, **G–C (3 H-bonds)**. Bases face the **interior** of the double helix, paired with bases on the opposite strand.
