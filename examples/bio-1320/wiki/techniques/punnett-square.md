# Punnett Square

## Summary
A grid that uses the **genotypes of two parents** to predict the **possible genotypes of their offspring** and their relative proportions.

**How to use** (monohybrid example, Tt × Tt):
1. Write one parent's gametes across the top (T, t) and the other's down the side (T, t).
2. Fill each cell by combining the row and column letters → TT, Tt, Tt, tt.
3. Count: 1 TT : 2 Tt : 1 tt → 3:1 phenotypic ratio (dominant : recessive).

**Sex-linked variant**: the X-bearing gametes carry one allele, but the Y-bearing male gamete has no allele — leading to the asymmetric inheritance patterns of [[inheritance/x-linked-recessive]] disorders.

The exam will only require **single-allele** (monohybrid) Punnett squares, including X-linked.
