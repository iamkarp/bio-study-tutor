# Semiconservative Replication

## Summary
When DNA replicates, **each daughter helix has one original (parental) strand and one newly synthesized strand**. This is the model proposed by Watson and Crick and confirmed by the Meselson–Stahl experiment (1958).

Contrasts with two alternatives:
- **Conservative** — original double helix stays together; new copy is entirely new.
- **Dispersive** — both daughter helices are mosaics of old and new pieces.

The semiconservative mechanism is built into the way DNA polymerase uses each parental strand as a template.
