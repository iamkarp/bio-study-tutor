# Metaphase I vs. metaphase II vs. mitotic metaphase

## Answer

| Phase | What lines up at the equator | Pairing? | Result of next phase |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Metaphase I** (meiosis) | Homologous chromosome **pairs** (each as duplicated chromosome) | Paired (synapsed), with chiasmata | Homologues separate (anaphase I) |
| **Metaphase II** (meiosis) | Single duplicated chromosomes | Not paired | Sister chromatids separate |
| **Mitotic metaphase** | Single duplicated chromosomes | Not paired | Sister chromatids separate |

**Similarities**:
- All three line chromosomes up at the cell equator with spindle microtubules attached at kinetochores.

**Key differences**:
- Only metaphase I has **paired homologues** (and only metaphase I features the random orientation that drives independent assortment).
- Metaphase II and mitotic metaphase look almost identical mechanically, but metaphase II starts from a haploid cell while mitotic metaphase starts from a diploid cell.
