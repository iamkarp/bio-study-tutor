# Outcome of meiosis I

## Answer
Meiosis I separates **homologous pairs** of chromosomes.

Worked example — start with **23 pairs (= 46 chromosomes, diploid)**. After **DNA replication** in S phase, you still have 46 chromosomes but each chromosome now consists of two sister chromatids.
- After **meiosis I**: **2 daughter cells**, each with **23 chromosomes** (each still as sister chromatids — duplicated, but only one of each homologous pair).

The cells are **haploid (n = 23)** in chromosome count, but DNA content per cell equals 2× the haploid (because chromatids haven't separated yet).
