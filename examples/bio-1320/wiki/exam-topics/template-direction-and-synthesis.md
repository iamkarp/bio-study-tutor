# Template direction and new-strand synthesis

## Answer
- **Template strand is read 3' → 5'**.
- **New strand is synthesized 5' → 3'** (DNA polymerase adds nucleotides only to the **3' end** of the growing chain).

**What determines directionality**: the **sugar's 3' carbon** (which carries an –OH group) and the **5' carbon** (which carries the phosphate). DNA polymerase requires a free **3'-OH** on the existing strand to attach the next nucleotide via a phosphodiester bond. Without a free 3'-OH, no extension is possible — which is why a **primer** is required to start replication.
