# Three components of a nucleotide; helix interior vs. exterior

## Answer
A DNA nucleotide is built from three parts:
1. A **phosphate group** (PO₄⁻)
2. A **5-carbon sugar** — **deoxyribose** in DNA (ribose in RNA)
3. A **nitrogenous base** — A, T, G, or C

In the **double helix**:
- **Sugar–phosphate backbone faces the EXTERIOR** (held together by phosphodiester bonds; charged, hydrophilic).
- **Nitrogenous bases face the INTERIOR**, paired with bases on the opposite strand by hydrogen bonds (the "rungs of the ladder").
