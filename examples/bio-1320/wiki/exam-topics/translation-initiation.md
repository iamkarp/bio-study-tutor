# Translation initiation

## Answer
**Pre-initiation complex** forms when the **small ribosomal subunit** binds to the 5' end of the mRNA (via the cap). The complex scans the mRNA until it finds the **start codon (AUG)**.

The **initiator tRNA**, which already carries **methionine**, has an **anticodon (3'-UAC-5')** that complementary base-pairs with the AUG. Once paired, the **large ribosomal subunit** joins, completing the ribosome with the initiator tRNA in the **P site**. Translation is now ready to elongate.

**Sequence of events**:
1. Pre-initiation complex (small subunit + initiator tRNA) binds mRNA at 5' cap
2. Complex scans until finding AUG; initiator tRNA pairs anticodon to it
3. Large subunit binds → ribosome assembled
