# Purpose of PCR; doubling per cycle

## Answer
**Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)** rapidly **amplifies** a specific DNA sequence — taking a tiny amount (even a few molecules) and producing **billions of copies** in a few hours. It's used in forensics, paternity testing, medical diagnostics, genetic engineering, and basic research.

**Doubling rule**: each PCR cycle **doubles** the number of target DNA copies.
- After cycle 1: **2 copies** (2¹)
- After cycle 2: **4 copies** (2²)
- After cycle 3: **8 copies** (2³)
- After cycle n: **2ⁿ copies**

So 30 cycles gives ~10⁹ copies — enough to visualize on a gel.

PCR was invented by **[[people/kary-mullis|Kary Mullis]]**, who won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for it.
