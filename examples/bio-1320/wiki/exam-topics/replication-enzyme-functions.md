# DNA replication enzymes — what each one does

## Answer

| Enzyme/Protein | Function |
|---|---|
| **[[enzymes/dna-helicase|Helicase]]** | Unwinds the double helix at the replication fork by breaking hydrogen bonds between base pairs |
| **[[enzymes/single-stranded-binding-protein|Single-stranded binding proteins (SSBs)]]** | Coat the unwound single strands to prevent them from re-annealing |
| **[[enzymes/topoisomerase|Topoisomerase]]** | Relieves the twisting/supercoiling tension created ahead of the replication fork |
| **[[enzymes/rna-primase|RNA primase]]** | Synthesizes a short **RNA primer** (~5–10 nt) on the template — DNA polymerase needs a free 3'-OH to extend, primase can start from scratch |
| **[[enzymes/dna-polymerase-iii|DNA polymerase III]]** | Adds DNA nucleotides to the 3' end of the primer, building the new strand 5' → 3'. Has **proofreading** ability (1 error per 10 million bases after proofreading) |
| **[[enzymes/dna-polymerase-i|DNA polymerase I]]** | Replaces the RNA primer with DNA nucleotides |
| **[[enzymes/dna-ligase|DNA ligase]]** | Seals the gaps between DNA fragments (joins Okazaki fragments on the lagging strand by forming the final phosphodiester bond) |
