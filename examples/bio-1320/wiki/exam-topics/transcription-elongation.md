# Transcription elongation

## Answer
During **elongation**, RNA polymerase moves along the **template strand** of DNA and builds an RNA copy by adding nucleotides to the **3' end** of the growing RNA strand (synthesis is **5' → 3'**, just like DNA replication). Each incoming RNA nucleotide pairs with its complementary base on the template (A-U, T-A, G-C, C-G).

**Only one** of the two DNA strands is copied — the template strand. The other (coding/sense) strand is not transcribed in that gene. Different genes can use different strands as their template.

The resulting RNA is **complementary** to the template strand, so its sequence matches the **coding** DNA strand (with U instead of T).
