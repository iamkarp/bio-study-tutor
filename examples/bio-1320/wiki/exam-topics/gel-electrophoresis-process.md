# Gel electrophoresis — visualizing PCR products

## Answer
**Gel electrophoresis** separates DNA fragments **by size** so they can be visualized.

**Process**:
1. **Load DNA** (e.g. PCR products) into wells at one end of an **agarose gel** (a porous polymer).
2. **Apply electric current** across the gel. DNA's phosphate backbone is **negatively charged**, so DNA migrates toward the **positive electrode**.
3. **Smaller fragments move faster** through the gel mesh; larger fragments move more slowly. Over a fixed time, fragments separate into **bands by size**.
4. **Stain** the gel (e.g. with ethidium bromide or SYBR safe) and image under UV light. Each band represents fragments of one size.

For STR profiling, the band pattern is the **DNA profile** — the positions of bands depend on how many repeats each STR allele has. Comparing profiles from a crime scene to a suspect tells you whether they match.
