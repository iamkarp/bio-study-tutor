# Describe the four phases of meiosis I

## Answer
1. **Prophase I** — Homologous chromosomes pair up (synapsis); chiasmata form where non-sister chromatids exchange DNA segments ([[processes/crossing-over]] → genetic recombination). Spindle microtubules assemble; nuclear envelope breaks down. _This is the longest and most distinctive phase._
2. **Metaphase I** — Paired homologous chromosomes line up at the cell equator. Each homologue is attached to the **opposite pole** by spindle microtubules. Maternal/paternal orientation is **random** — this is where [[principles/law-of-independent-assortment]] takes effect.
3. **Anaphase I** — **Homologous chromosomes** (not sister chromatids!) separate and move to opposite poles. Each pole gets one duplicated member from each homologous pair. _This is the key difference from mitotic anaphase._
4. **Telophase I** — Two haploid clusters of duplicated chromosomes form. Spindle disappears; cytokinesis splits the cell. Nuclear envelopes may re-form. Chromosomes do **not** decondense or replicate before meiosis II.
