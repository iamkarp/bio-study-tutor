# Gregor Mendel (1822–1884)

## Summary
Augustinian friar in Brno (now Czech Republic) who founded the science of genetics through his **pea plant experiments** in the 1850s–60s. By carefully crossing true-breeding lines and counting offspring phenotypes, he deduced the laws of segregation and independent assortment decades before chromosomes or DNA were known. His 1866 paper was largely ignored until 1900.
