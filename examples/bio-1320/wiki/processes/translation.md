# Translation

## Summary
The process by which a [[structures/ribosome]] reads an [[molecules/mrna]] template in 3-nucleotide codons and assembles a corresponding protein. Catalysis is performed by the [[molecules/rrna]] in the large ribosomal subunit (a ribozyme). Occurs in the **cytoplasm** (free ribosomes) or on the rough ER.

## Steps
1. [[processes/translation-initiation]] — small ribosomal subunit binds mRNA, scans to start codon (AUG); initiator tRNA carrying [[molecules/methionine]] base-pairs; large subunit joins.
2. [[processes/translation-elongation]] — second tRNA enters A site; peptide bond forms between amino acids; ribosome shifts one codon.
3. [[processes/translation-termination]] — ribosome reaches stop codon; release factor binds; protein released.

## Key Players
- [[molecules/mrna]] — the message
- [[molecules/trna]] — the adaptors carrying amino acids
- [[molecules/rrna]] — structure + catalysis of [[structures/ribosome]]
- [[principles/genetic-code]] — codon → amino acid mapping
