# Transcription

## Summary
The process of synthesizing an RNA copy of a gene from a DNA template. Occurs in the **nucleus** (eukaryotes) or cytoplasm (prokaryotes). Catalyzed by [[enzymes/rna-polymerase]]. The product can be [[molecules/mrna]] (later translated to protein), [[molecules/trna]], [[molecules/rrna]], or other RNAs.

## Steps
1. [[processes/transcription-initiation]] — RNA polymerase binds the [[structures/promoter]]; in eukaryotes [[structures/transcription-factor|transcription factors]] are required.
2. [[processes/transcription-elongation]] — RNA polymerase reads the template strand 3' → 5' and synthesizes RNA 5' → 3'. Only one DNA strand is copied.
3. [[processes/transcription-termination]] — at the [[structures/terminator]], everything dissociates.

In eukaryotes, the transcript ([[concepts/pre-mrna]]) undergoes [[processes/rna-processing]] before leaving the nucleus.
