# Meiosis II

## Summary
The second meiotic division. **Meiosis II separates sister chromatids** (mechanically similar to mitosis) but starts from haploid cells already produced by meiosis I. **No DNA replication** between meiosis I and meiosis II.

## Steps
1. [[processes/prophase-ii]] — spindle re-forms; chromatids attach to opposite poles
2. [[processes/metaphase-ii]] — duplicated chromosomes line up singly at the equator
3. [[processes/anaphase-ii]] — sister chromatids separate
4. [[processes/telophase-ii]] — four haploid cells form, each with single (unduplicated) chromosomes

## Outcome
**4 haploid cells** total (across the two cells from meiosis I), each genetically unique due to crossing over and independent assortment in meiosis I.
