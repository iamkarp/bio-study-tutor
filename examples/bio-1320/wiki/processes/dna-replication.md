# DNA Replication

## Summary
The process of copying the entire DNA of a cell before division. Replication is **[[principles/semiconservative-replication|semiconservative]]**: each daughter helix has one original (template) strand and one newly synthesized strand. Occurs during [[processes/s-phase]] of interphase.

## Mechanism
1. **[[enzymes/dna-helicase]]** unwinds the double helix at the [[structures/origin-of-replication]], creating a [[structures/replication-fork]].
2. **[[enzymes/single-stranded-binding-protein]]** keeps the single strands separated.
3. **[[enzymes/topoisomerase]]** relieves twisting tension ahead of the fork.
4. **[[enzymes/rna-primase]]** lays down a short RNA primer with a free 3'-OH.
5. **[[enzymes/dna-polymerase-iii]]** extends the primer 5' → 3', adding nucleotides complementary to the template (A-T, G-C).
6. The **[[structures/leading-strand]]** is synthesized continuously toward the fork. The **[[structures/lagging-strand]]** is synthesized away from the fork in [[structures/okazaki-fragment|Okazaki fragments]].
7. **[[enzymes/dna-polymerase-i]]** replaces RNA primers with DNA.
8. **[[enzymes/dna-ligase]]** seals the gaps between fragments.

## Key Properties
- **Direction of synthesis**: 5' → 3' (template read 3' → 5')
- **Speed**: bacteria ~1000 nt/sec; eukaryotes ~50 nt/sec
- **Accuracy after proofreading**: ~1 mistake per 10⁷ nucleotides
- **Prokaryotes** use one [[structures/origin-of-replication]] on a circular chromosome; **eukaryotes** use many on linear chromosomes ([[comparisons/prokaryote-vs-eukaryote-replication]])
- The lagging strand of linear chromosomes can't replicate to the very end → [[structures/telomere|telomere]] shortening (rescued by [[enzymes/telomerase]] in some cells)
