# Mitosis

## Summary
The form of cell division that produces **two genetically identical diploid daughter cells** from one diploid parent cell. Used for growth, tissue repair, and asexual reproduction in single-celled eukaryotes. Like meiosis, it is preceded by one round of [[processes/dna-replication]] in [[processes/s-phase]].

## Compared To
- [[processes/meiosis]] — see [[comparisons/mitosis-vs-meiosis]] for the full side-by-side
- Mitotic anaphase separates **sister chromatids** (like meiosis II), not homologues (like meiosis I).
